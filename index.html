GSORB-SLAM: Gaussian Splatting SLAM benefits from ORB features and Transmittance information

--

Paper Code

Overview

In this paper, we present GSORB-SLAM, a dense SLAM framework that integrates 3DGS with ORB features through a tightly-coupled optimization pipeline. To address the impact of noise and artifacts, we propose a novel geometric representation and optimization method for tracking, which significantly improves localization accuracy and robustness. For high-fidelity mapping, we propose an adaptive Gaussian expansion and regularization method, which enables compact yet expressive scene modeling while suppressing redundant primitives. Furthermore, a hybrid graph-based viewpoint selection mechanism is designed to dynamically balance scene coverage and optimization efficiency, effectively mitigating over-fitting and accelerating convergence. Extensive evaluations on different datasets demonstrate that our system achieves state-of-the-art performance in both tracking precision (improving RMSE by 16.2\% compared to ORB-SLAM2 baselines) and reconstruction quality (improving PSNR by 3.84 dB compared to 3DGS-SLAM baselines).
